Dating Safety Guide

Your safety is our priority. Follow these guidelines to ensure a secure and enjoyable online dating experience.

Safety First

At Connecting Hearts, we are committed to creating a safe environment for our users. While we implement robust security measures on our platform, it's also important for you to follow safety best practices when connecting with others online and meeting in person.

This comprehensive guide provides tips and resources to help you stay safe throughout your dating journey. We encourage all users to review these guidelines before engaging with others on our platform.

Emergency Resources

In case of immediate danger, call your local emergency number:

UK: 999

US: 911

Australia: 000

To report concerning behavior on Connecting Hearts, use the "Report" button or contact our safety team at safety@connectinghearts.com.

Before You Meet

Take Time to Get to Know Each Other

Before meeting in person, spend time communicating through our platform to establish a level of comfort and trust. Use our messaging and video chat features to have meaningful conversations and look for consistency in their communication.

Be wary of users who seem eager to move the conversation off the platform immediately or who push for a face-to-face meeting too quickly.

Verify Their Identity

Look for profiles with verified badges, which indicate users who have completed our verification process. Additionally, consider having a video chat before meeting in person to confirm they match their profile photos.

Be cautious of profiles with only one photo, very limited information, or that seem too perfect. If something seems suspicious, trust your instincts.

Research Before Meeting

It's okay to do some basic research on a potential match before meeting them. Looking up public social media profiles can help verify that they are who they claim to be.

However, respect privacy boundaries and remember that not everyone has a strong online presence.

Watch for Red Flags

Be alert to potential warning signs such as:

  • Inconsistencies in their stories or profile information
  • Refusing video calls or always having excuses not to meet
  • Asking for financial assistance or gifts
  • Overly controlling behavior or pressuring you for personal information
  • Making you uncomfortable by moving too quickly in the relationship

If you notice these or other concerning behaviors, use the "Report" function to notify our safety team.

Planning the First Meeting

Meet in a Public Place

Always arrange your first few meetings in busy, public locations such as cafes, restaurants, or well-populated parks. Avoid remote areas, private homes, or secluded spots for initial meetings.

Choose locations where staff or other people are present throughout your meeting.

Arrange Your Own Transportation

Plan to arrive and leave independently. Avoid being picked up or dropped off at your home until you've established trust over multiple meetings. Having your own transportation gives you the freedom to leave if you feel uncomfortable.

If using public transportation, make sure services are running at the time you plan to leave.

Tell Someone About Your Plans

Inform a friend or family member about your date plans, including:

  • Who you're meeting (share their profile or picture if possible)
  • Where you're going
  • When you expect to return

Consider arranging a check-in call or text during or after the date.

Stay Sober and Alert

Keep your wits about you by limiting alcohol consumption, especially during first meetings. Never leave your food or drinks unattended, and be suspicious if someone is encouraging you to drink more than you're comfortable with.

If you feel disoriented or unusually intoxicated, seek help from venue staff immediately.

During and After the Date

Trust Your Instincts

If something doesn't feel right, it probably isn't. You don't need a specific reason to end a date earlyâ€”your comfort and safety come first. Have an exit strategy prepared, such as a friend calling with an "emergency" or a pre-arranged time you need to leave.

Never feel obligated to stay in a situation that makes you uncomfortable.

Maintain Privacy

Be cautious about sharing personal information during early meetings. Avoid discussing details like your home address, workplace location, financial information, or daily routines until trust is established.

Be particularly careful about sharing information that could compromise your online accounts or identity.

Report Concerning Behavior

If someone behaves inappropriately or makes you feel unsafe, report them to Connecting Hearts. This helps us maintain a safe community for all users. You can report users through their profile or by contacting our safety team directly.

In cases of threatening behavior or harassment, also consider reporting to local authorities.

Take Things at Your Own Pace

There's no need to rush into a physical or emotional relationship. A respectful partner will understand your boundaries and be patient. Communication about comfort levels and expectations is key to building a healthy relationship.

Be wary of anyone who pressures you to move faster than you're ready for.

Pre-Date Safety Checklist

I've communicated with this person enough to feel comfortable meeting them
I've verified their identity through video chat or other means
I've chosen a public place for our meeting
I've arranged my own transportation to and from the date
I've told a friend or family member about my plans
I have a fully charged phone with me
I've planned an exit strategy if I feel uncomfortable
I know how to report concerns on the Connecting Hearts platform

Our Safety Commitments

At Connecting Hearts, we're dedicated to maintaining a safe platform for all users. Here's how we help protect you:

  1. Profile Verification - We offer verification options to confirm users' identities.
  2. Secure Messaging - Our messaging system keeps your personal contact information private until you choose to share it.
  3. Report System - We make it easy to report suspicious or inappropriate behavior.
  4. Safety Reviews - Our team reviews reports promptly and takes appropriate action.
  5. Education - We provide ongoing safety information through our blog, emails, and platform notifications.
  6. Moderation - We use both automated systems and human reviewers to monitor for suspicious activity.

Report a Concern

If you encounter any behavior that makes you uncomfortable or seems suspicious, please report it immediately. You can report users directly from their profile by clicking the "Report" button, or contact our safety team at safety@connectinghearts.com.

All reports are handled confidentially and investigated thoroughly. Your safety is our top priority, and we appreciate your help in maintaining a secure environment for all users.
